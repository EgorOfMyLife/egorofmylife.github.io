1. In Lauren Schwulst's opinion an individual website can be a way for self-expression that is much richer in comparison with social media platforms. A website can have many different uses but it has to be the user who decides what "language, style, rules, and architecture" the website will have. I would say that I agree with the thesis. It is highly disturbing that more and more these days people are cut off from self-expression through web design (or coding in general) because of the overwhelming usage of social media. I have recently told my art buddies that I'm taking a web design class and the first thing I heard from them was: "Why?" It was surprising to them that I considered such an avenue of knowledge. Our days it seems that even creative people sometimes don't entertain a thought about coding as an engaging artistic process, it is understandable that the average Instagram dweller will never wonder about the HTML structure of said Instagram.
